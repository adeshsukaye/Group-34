Frequently Asked Questions

1.What is digital farming?

The term ‘digital farming’ refers to technologies and trends being used to change the way growers control their farm operations and analyze their yield performance. It covers a broad range of areas such as soil sensing, irrigation control, farm management, machinery, and crop lifecycle management. For instance, sensors are showing growers new insights into soil conditions, yield performance and crop health. And automation is making it easier to control crucial things like irrigation systems remotely from your phone.

2.Which kinds of farmers will benefit most from digital farming?

Digital farming gives farmers a better view and understanding of their soil, their crops, and their equipment.

3.What are the basic components of digital farming?

Digital farming can be broken up into three different areas: precision, decision, and cultivation. Precision uses GPS geo-location services and extensions to sense, identify, and act on predictive decision making. Decision uses quality information that is timely and within the decision loop. This uses advanced sensors, descriptive models, and predictive algorithms to provide insight to crops, equipment, and other challenges. And cultivation uses end-to-end, real-time, continuous decision processes, as mentioned above. It looks at planting and harvesting, currently through the observational power of the farmer itself. In the future, it will look at things through the digital ecosystem. All three of these work together to deliver real time insights to farmers about their crops, land, and equipment.

4.How can digital farming help me increase my yields?

Digital farming is all about giving farmers real-time insights into their farm so they can analyze and make optimal decisions for their farms. Using it can help farmers optimize irrigation systems and strategies, weather data, soil and plant data, and more.

5.What is farming?

According to the Dictionary definition, “Farming is the act or process of working the ground, planting seeds, and growing edible plants. You can also describe raising animals for milk or meat as farming… Farming is a great way to describe the lifestyle and work of people whose jobs are in the agriculture industry.” The noun “farm,” which derives from the Medieval Latin Firma, originally meant, “A lease on farm land.”

6.What is the value of United States agricultural exports and US agricultural imports?

According to the most recent USDA data, the value of agricultural exports reached $129.7 billion in 2016. During the same time period, the US imported $113.1 billion worth of agricultural imports, producing a surplus of more than $16 billion.

7.What are the leading farm commodities, in terms of cash receipts, in the United States?

According to the latest data from USDA, the top 10 farm commodities by revenue are: Cattle/calves Corn,Soybeans, Dairy products/milk,Broilers Hogs,Miscellaneous crops,Chicken eggs,Wheat Hay.

8.How important is agriculture to the overall economy?

Agriculture makes up about 4.5% of the nominal Gross Domestic Product of the United States. Though, in many other countries, agriculture contributes 5-10 times as much to the GDP.

9.Why is agriculture important?

Agriculture plays a crucial role in the economy of developing countries, and provides the main source of food, income, and employment to rural populations. For many countries, agriculture is a leading economic source, with those countries helping to provide some of the US’s food as well.

10.What is indoor farming?

Indoor farming is the practice and process of growing crops and produce in vertically stacked layers. These layers use a controlled-environment agriculture (CEA), which optimizes plant growth by using soilless farming techniques.

11.What are the benefits of indoor farming?

Benefits of indoor farming include the ability to grow a wide variety of crops, regardless of season; providing jobs and fresh food for family, friends, and your community; and avoiding common farming dangers like chemical exposure, weather condition hazards, and pests/insects.

12.What are the disadvantages of indoor farming?

The disadvantages of indoor farming include being slightly expensive, due to equipment and technology, and using a lot of electricity.

13.What is vertical farming?

Vertical farming is the practice and process of growing crops and produce within a vertically stacked layer. As a type of indoor farming, vertical farming uses CEA or controlled environment agriculture, which helps to optimize plant growth by using different techniques like hydroponics, aquaponics, and aeroponics.

14.What is a greenhouse?

A greenhouse is a building with glass walls and ceiling/roof that are used to grow plants. Greenhouses are able to stay warm inside using sunlight to warm both the plants and the air within. Sunlight is also used for photosynthesis, though supplemental artificial lighting can also be used.

15.What are the benefits of a greenhouse?

Benefits of a greenhouse include having fresh greens, vegetables, and fruit; a longer growing season; protection from pest and insects; and the ability to grow items that aren’t available using traditional means or during off seasons.

16.What are the disadvantages of a greenhouse?

Some disadvantages of a greenhouse include choosing a proper location that will be able to capture the sunlight during all seasons and temperature and ventilation management, so crops don’t die, trapping pests or diseases within.

17.What is a container farm?

A container farm is an indoor micro-farm built inside a repurposed shipping container. The containers used for these farms are typically intermodal, meaning they’re built to be used with a variety of transportation modes. Container farms typically use hydroponic and vertical farming methods to grow crops.

18.What does the future of farming look like?

Food concerns have motivated both consumers and scientists to look at other means of agriculture. Consumers, especially, are growing more and more interested in knowing where their food, especially produce and fruits, are coming from. For example, grocery shoppers look for labels indicating foods are locally grown or sourced. The increase of technology within farming has opened the door for more opportunity for growers and consumers.

19.What are GMOs?

“A GMO, or genetically modified organism, is a plant, animal, microorganism or other organism whose genetic makeup has been modified in a laboratory using genetic engineering or transgenic technology,” according to the Non-GMO Project. In GMOs, genes appear that aren’t present in nature or through traditional crossbreeding. The safety of GMOs are unknown.

20.What does it mean to be an “Organic” farm?

Organic farms use pest controls with natural ingredients and biological fertilizers, rather than synthetic ones. The goal of organic farming is to combat negative effects of chemicals used in farming. Organic farms use fewer pesticides, reduce soil erosion, decrease nitrate infecting water, and recycle animal waste.
